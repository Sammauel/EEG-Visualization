What is EEG?

An electroencephalogram (EEG) is a test that detects electrical activity in your brain using small, flat metal discs (electrodes) attached to your scalp.

Your brain cells communicate via electrical impulses and are active all the time, even when you're asleep.

These test can provide information on potentially discovering if the patient has a brain disorder.

Why should we care what happens when we sleep?

The focus of this project was being able to represent a visualization of EEG recording that a professional will be able to look at and analyze potential spindles in a patient while he or she is sleeping during the recording period.

Sleep spindles are periods where the brain is inhibiting processing to keep the sleeper in a tranquil state. Some of the conditions into what classifies a spindle is that while they may or may not be synchronous but have to be symmetrical and bilateral.
